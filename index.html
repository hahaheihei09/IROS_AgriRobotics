Welcome to IROS Agrirobotics 2025

Agricultural robotics is at the forefront of addressing global food security challenges, yet its full potential remains untapped due to the complex interplay between design, control, and interaction with living plants. This workshop bridges these domains, offering a platform for interdisciplinary dialogue.

The workshop will focus on three interconnected themes: design (e.g., biomimetic mechanisms, soft robotics for delicate manipulation), control (e.g., adaptive algorithms for unstructured environments, AI-driven decision-making), and plant-robot interaction (e.g., robotic phenotyping, canopy manipulation, and growth stimulation). By integrating these themes, the workshop will explore how robots can not only perform tasks in agricultural settings but also interact intelligently and symbiotically with plants.

This focus is both timely and distinctive. While previous workshops have often addressed design and control in isolation, this event uniquely emphasizes the emerging field of plant-robot interaction, which has seen growing interest due to its potential to revolutionize precision agriculture. Topics such as robotic harvesting in dense clusters, real-time plant health monitoring, and tactile stimulation for growth optimization will be highlighted, offering fresh perspectives and fostering cross-disciplinary collaboration.

The workshop will feature invited talks from leading experts, lightning presentations by early-career researchers, and interactive poster sessions. A panel discussion will address grand challenges and future directions, encouraging open dialogue between academia, industry, and policymakers. By combining cutting-edge research with practical applications, the workshop aims to inspire new ideas, foster collaborations, and accelerate the development of sustainable agricultural robotics.
